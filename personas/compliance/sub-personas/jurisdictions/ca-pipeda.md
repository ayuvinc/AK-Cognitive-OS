## Overview

Canada has a dual-track privacy framework:
- **PIPEDA** (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) — federal law governing private sector organisations in commercial activities, and inter-provincial/international data flows
- **Quebec Law 25** (formally Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector, as amended by Bill 64) — significantly strengthened Quebec's provincial privacy law, now closer to GDPR in scope
- **Provincial laws** (Alberta PIPA, BC PIPA) — substantially similar laws for activities within those provinces

Enforced by: **Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC)** (federal); provincial commissioners for provincial laws.

## PIPEDA — Key Principles (10 Fair Information Principles)

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| Accountability | Organisation responsible for personal information in its custody |
| Identifying purposes | State purposes before or at time of collection |
| Consent | Obtain meaningful consent for collection, use, disclosure |
| Limiting collection | Collect only what is necessary |
| Limiting use, disclosure, retention | Use only for identified purposes; retain only as long as necessary |
| Accuracy | Keep personal information accurate, complete, up-to-date |
| Safeguards | Protect with appropriate security |
| Openness | Privacy policies and practices readily available |
| Individual access | Individuals can access their personal information |
| Challenging compliance | Individuals can challenge compliance |

---

## Consent Under PIPEDA

- Express consent required for sensitive information
- Implied consent acceptable for less sensitive information in some circumstances
- Consent can be withdrawn at any time (subject to legal/contractual restrictions)
- Children's consent: generally requires parental consent for children under 13

## Quebec Law 25 (Bill 64) — Key Changes Effective 2023

Quebec Law 25 brought Quebec's framework closest to GDPR-level requirements:

**Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs):** Required before any technology project involving personal information, and for any communication outside Quebec.

**Privacy by default:** Products must be configured to provide the highest level of privacy protection by default.

**Consent:** Must be clear, free, and informed. Bundled consent is invalid. Forms must be easy to understand.

**Data portability:** Individuals have the right to receive their personal information in a technology-neutral format.

**Right to de-indexing:** Individuals can request removal of their personal information from online publication if it causes them harm.

**Breach notification:** Must notify the Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI) and affected individuals for breaches presenting a risk of serious injury.

**Privacy Officer:** Mandatory appointment and publication of name/title.

**Cross-border transfers:** Require prior PIA; transfers permitted only to jurisdictions with equivalent protection or with contractual protections.

---

## Breach Notification (PIPEDA + Quebec)

**PIPEDA:** Notify OPC and affected individuals when breach presents a real risk of significant harm. No specific time limit (ASAP guidance).

**Quebec Law 25:** Notify CAI within 72 hours; notify individuals if risk of serious injury.

---

## Cross-Border Transfers

PIPEDA: Transfers to third parties in other countries require contractual protections comparable to PIPEDA.
Quebec Law 25: PIA required; transfers permitted only if adequate protection confirmed.

## Framework Reference Checklist (Canada)

- [ ] PIPEDA applicability confirmed (commercial activity in Canada or cross-provincial data flows)
- [ ] Quebec Law 25 applicability confirmed (Quebec residents or operations in Quebec)
- [ ] Privacy Officer appointed and contact published (Quebec Law 25 requirement)
- [ ] Consent mechanism meaningful and purpose-specific
- [ ] Privacy policy covers all 10 PIPEDA principles
- [ ] Individual access rights mechanism in place
- [ ] PIA conducted for technology projects (Quebec) or cross-border transfers (Quebec)
- [ ] Privacy by default implemented (Quebec)
- [ ] Breach notification procedure covers PIPEDA + Quebec timelines
- [ ] Cross-border transfer contracts in place (PIPEDA) + PIA (Quebec)
