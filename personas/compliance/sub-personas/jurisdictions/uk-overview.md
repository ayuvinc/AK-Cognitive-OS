## Overview

Post-Brexit, the UK has its own data protection framework:
- **UK GDPR** — the retained EU GDPR as modified by the Data Protection, Privacy and Electronic
  Communications (Amendments etc.) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019
- **Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018)** — supplements UK GDPR and covers law enforcement processing
- Enforced by the **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)**

UK GDPR is substantially similar to EU GDPR but is a separate legal instrument.
Compliance with EU GDPR does not automatically mean UK GDPR compliance.

---

## Key Differences from EU GDPR

| Area | UK GDPR / DPA 2018 |
|---|---|
| Lawful basis | Same 6 bases as EU GDPR (Article 6 equivalent) |
| Legitimate interests | Slightly broader flexibility for public interest tasks |
| Age of consent | 13 years old (lower than EU GDPR's 16-year default) |
| International transfers | UK adequacy decisions separate from EU — UK has its own list |
| ICO registration | Controllers must register with ICO (fee applies unless exempt) |
| Fines | Up to £17.5 million or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever higher) |

---

## Lawful Bases (UK GDPR Article 6)

Same 6 bases as EU GDPR:
Consent / Contract / Legal obligation / Vital interests / Public task / Legitimate interests

---

## Data Subject Rights (UK GDPR)

Same rights as EU GDPR — access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection.
Response time: within one calendar month. UK residents have these rights regardless of
where the data controller is located (if offering goods/services to UK residents).

---

## PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003)

PECR covers:
- Cookies and similar tracking technologies (consent required, with exceptions)
- Electronic marketing (email, SMS — consent required)
- Security of electronic communications services

**Cookie rules:** Cookies that are not strictly necessary require prior informed consent.
Analytics cookies = require consent. Session cookies = exempt.

ICO guidance: Cookie consent must be as easy to refuse as to accept.
Pre-ticked boxes are not valid consent.

---

## International Transfers (Post-Brexit)

UK-to-EU/EEA transfers: UK has recognised EU/EEA as adequate (as of early 2026 — verify currency).
EU/EEA-to-UK transfers: EU Commission granted adequacy for the UK (subject to ongoing review).
UK-to-other-countries: UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or UK Addendum to EU SCCs.
UK-to-US: Requires IDTA or UK Addendum; Privacy Shield equivalent (UK-US Data Bridge) exists — verify current status.

---

## ICO Registration

Most data controllers must register with the ICO and pay an annual fee.
Exemptions include small organisations processing only for staff admin, accounts, etc.
Failure to register is a criminal offence.

---

## Breach Notification

- Notify ICO: within 72 hours of becoming aware (if risk to individuals' rights/freedoms)
- Notify individuals: without undue delay if high risk
- Document all breaches, including those not reported

---

## Framework Reference Checklist (UK)

- [ ] UK GDPR applicability confirmed (established in UK or targeting UK residents)
- [ ] Lawful basis identified for each processing activity
- [ ] Privacy notice covers all required elements (UK GDPR Article 13/14 equivalent)
- [ ] ICO registration completed (if required)
- [ ] PECR cookie consent in place (if using non-essential cookies)
- [ ] Electronic marketing consent obtained (if sending marketing emails/SMS)
- [ ] Data subject rights mechanisms implemented (1-month response)
- [ ] International transfer mechanisms in place (if transferring outside UK)
- [ ] Breach notification procedure documented (72-hour ICO notification)
- [ ] DPA 2018 special category conditions reviewed (if processing special category data)
